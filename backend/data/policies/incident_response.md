# Incident Response Policy

## 1. Purpose
Establish procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

## 2. Scope
All security events affecting organizational systems, data, or personnel.

## 3. Incident Categories

| Severity | Description | Response Time |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Critical | Active breach, ransomware, data exfiltration | 15 minutes |
| High | Confirmed compromise, malware outbreak | 1 hour |
| Medium | Suspicious activity, policy violation | 4 hours |
| Low | Minor anomaly, informational | 24 hours |

## 4. Response Phases

### 4.1 Preparation
- Maintain IR team contact list
- Conduct quarterly tabletop exercises
- Maintain forensic tools and procedures

### 4.2 Detection & Analysis
- Monitor SIEM alerts
- Validate and triage incidents
- Determine scope and impact

### 4.3 Containment
- Isolate affected systems
- Preserve evidence
- Implement temporary controls

### 4.4 Eradication
- Remove threat actor access
- Patch exploited vulnerabilities
- Reset compromised credentials

### 4.5 Recovery
- Restore from clean backups
- Verify system integrity
- Monitor for re-compromise

### 4.6 Post-Incident
- Conduct lessons learned
- Update procedures
- Report to management

## 5. Reporting
All suspected incidents must be reported to the Security Operations Center immediately.

## 6. Review
This policy is reviewed annually and after every major incident.
